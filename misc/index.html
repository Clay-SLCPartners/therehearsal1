The Nathan Fielder Method

In HBO's "The Rehearsal," Nathan Fielder helps people prepare for life's crucial moments through obsessive recreation and practice. We're applying that same philosophy to mental health conversations.

🔄

Repetition Builds Confidence

Just as Nathan rehearses conversations dozens of times, you can practice mental health discussions until the words feel natural. No more freezing up when it matters most.

"I rehearsed this conversation 47 times. By the 48th, I knew exactly what to say."

🎬

Every Variable Considered

Nathan maps out every possible response. Our scenarios include 4 choices per interaction, preparing you for defensive reactions, emotional breakdowns, or unexpected support.

"What if they cry? What if they get angry? What if they hug you? I had a plan for everything."

🏗️

Controlled Environment

Nathan builds entire sets to practice. While we can't rebuild your friend's apartment, we simulate the emotional environment of each conversation with painstaking detail.

"The fake apartment was identical. Even the coffee stain on the couch. Details matter."

America's Mental Healthcare Crisis

Millions of Americans live in mental healthcare deserts with no access to qualified professionals. The system is fundamentally broken, leaving those who need help the most without options.

🏜️

Mental Healthcare Deserts

160 million Americans live in designated Mental Health Professional Shortage Areas. Rural communities are hit hardest, with some areas having zero mental health providers for hundreds of miles.

8,000+

additional providers needed to meet basic demand

💸

Unaffordable Care

Therapy costs $100-250 per session without insurance. Even with insurance, many therapists don't accept it, and those who do often have months-long waitlists.

$4,800-12,000

average annual cost for weekly therapy

⚠️

Systemic Breakdown

The U.S. mental healthcare system is fundamentally underserved. Insurance companies deny coverage, providers burn out, and patients fall through the cracks.

of counties have no practicing psychiatrist

Deadly Wait Times

Average wait for a first appointment is 48+ days. In crisis situations, emergency rooms are often the only option, leading to traumatic and expensive interventions.

48-90 days

typical wait for mental health appointments

Rural America Abandoned

25+ million rural Americans live in areas with severe mental health professional shortages. Suicide rates in rural areas are nearly double those in urban areas.

rural Americans without mental health access

Insurance Obstacles

Many therapists don't accept insurance due to low reimbursement rates and administrative burden. Those who do often have full practices, leaving insured patients with few options.

🛡️ Protective Resources - You Don't Have To Accept Cruelty

🚫 Immediate Protection

  • "I'm not discussing this further."
  • "Your cruelty says more about you than me."
  • "I deserve better than this."
  • "I'm choosing my mental health over this conversation."

📋 Nathan's Documentation Tips

  • Screenshot cruel messages immediately
  • Note date, time, witnesses present
  • Save workplace harassment evidence
  • "I once documented 147 ways to say no" - Nathan

❤️ After-Encounter Self-Care

  • Remind yourself: "Their cruelty isn't about me"
  • Practice the 3.7-second calming breath
  • Reach out to supportive people
  • Block/limit contact when possible

"You don't owe anyone an explanation for your mental health needs. Boundaries are self-care, not selfishness." - The Rehearsal Team

Mental Health Resources

While rehearsals help you prepare, professional support is irreplaceable. Here are trusted resources for when you need real help.

🚨 Crisis Support - Available 24/7

National Suicide Prevention Lifeline

988

Call or text - Free, confidential support

Crisis Text Line

Text HOME to 741741

24/7 crisis counseling via text

The Trevor Project (LGBTQ+)

1-866-488-7386

Crisis support for LGBTQ+ youth

If you're in immediate danger, call 911 or go to your nearest emergency room.

National Mental Health Organizations

🏛️ National Institute of Mental Health (NIMH)

The lead federal agency for research on mental disorders. Provides authoritative information on mental health conditions, treatments, and research.

Website: nimh.nih.gov
Phone: 1-866-615-6464

Best for: Research-based information, understanding conditions, treatment options

🤝 National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI)

The nation's largest grassroots mental health organization. Offers support groups, education programs, and advocacy for people with mental illness and their families.

Website: nami.org
Helpline: 1-800-950-NAMI (6264)

Best for: Local support groups, family support, peer connections, advocacy

🌟 Mental Health America (MHA)

Leading community-based nonprofit dedicated to mental health promotion and illness prevention. Offers screening tools, educational resources, and advocacy.

Website: mhanational.org
Phone: 1-800-969-6642

Best for: Mental health screening, prevention resources, workplace mental health

🏥 SAMHSA Treatment Locator

Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration. Find mental health and substance use treatment facilities in your area.

Website: findtreatment.gov
Helpline: 1-800-662-4357

Best for: Finding local treatment providers, insurance navigation, treatment options

🔍 Psychology Today Therapist Directory

Comprehensive directory to find therapists, psychiatrists, and support groups in your area. Filter by insurance, specialty, and treatment approach.

Website: psychologytoday.com/us/therapists

Best for: Finding local therapists, reading therapist profiles, insurance matching

💻 Online Therapy Platforms

Remote therapy options for those in mental health deserts or who prefer online sessions. Multiple platforms available with licensed therapists.

Options: BetterHelp, Talkspace, MDLIVE, Cerebral
Cost: $60-120/week typically

Best for: Rural areas, busy schedules, anxiety about in-person therapy

Specialized Support

🌈 The Trevor Project (LGBTQ+)

Crisis intervention and suicide prevention for LGBTQ+ young people. 24/7 phone, chat, and text support.

Crisis Line: 1-866-488-7386
Text: START to 678-678
Website: thetrevorproject.org

🎖️ Veterans Crisis Line

24/7 support for veterans, service members, National Guard, Reserve, and their families and friends.

Crisis Line: 988, Press 1
Text: 838255
Website: veteranscrisisline.net

🤗 PFLAG (Family Support)

Support, education, and advocacy for families and friends of LGBTQ+ people. Local chapters nationwide.

Website: pflag.org
Phone: 1-202-467-8180

🤱 Postpartum Support International

Support for perinatal mood and anxiety disorders. Helpline, support groups, and provider directory.

Helpline: 1-800-944-4773
Website: postpartum.net
